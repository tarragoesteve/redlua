I started noticing that I ended more tired in my padel matches and I couldn't keep up trekking with my friends last year. What did change? Clearly I stopped running and this impacted my aerobic performance. I don't have the time nor the energy to start running at the volumes I did, but I needed a solution to increase my aerobic resistance.

After observing some activities on my smartwatch and what training effect they had on my body, I noticed that it was when I was hitting an anerobic HR (heart rate) that it improved my aerobic resistance. At first it did not made any sense but I started researching and found out this is the way our body works. I like to thing of my body as it has some kind of [plasticity](https://phys.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/University_Physics/University_Physics_(OpenStax)/Book%3A_University_Physics_I_-_Mechanics_Sound_Oscillations_and_Waves_(OpenStax)/12%3A_Static_Equilibrium_and_Elasticity/12.06%3A_Elasticity_and_Plasticity). When we go beyond our threshold, the body adapts to this new inputs so it can sustain them better the next time. In some sense it's the same as strength training, normally you do a small amount of repetitions with maximum weight.

That's why I decided to modify my strength training sessions to extend them with [HIIT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-intensity_interval_training) at the end of the session. On the first week I did only running intervals. Running after doing my legs days wasn't the smartest idea, as I was already with my legs tired when doing the sprints and I thought I would injure myself. So instead of it I decided to set up my days following this table:

|          | Monday | Tuesday | Thursday |
|----------|--------|---------|----------|
| Strength | Push   | Pull    | Legs     |
| HIT      | Run    | Cycle   | Row      |

After just 3 weeks I started noticing a difference in my soccer games, being able to withstand for longer. I might do another post documenting my strength exercises someday. Until then, let's keep pushing our elastic limits.
